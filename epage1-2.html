In Tang Dynasty(A.D.618~907) and Song Dynasty(A.D.960~1279): fake flowers, fake fruit, and flour miniatures were made as sacrificial offerings or put on a great banquet. In Song Dynasty(A.D.960~1279) and Yuan Dynasty(A.D.1206~1368): people made miniatures of human beings and animals to let guests appreciate in a feast. In the end of Ming Dynasty(A.D.1368~1644) and the beginning of Ching Dynasty(A.D.1644~1911): the master workers of making flour miniatures would carry a long box to go to a temple fair at the end of lunar year.

Now making the flour miniature in China is also popular. The applications are not only the sacrificial offerings but also lucky gifts of marriage, funeral, birthday, and festival in folk daily life. So the flour miniature can be worshiped, given, and appreciated, and eaten.

In Taiwan, the art of the flour miniature has degenerated as a result of the changing environment. The young generation wouldn't like to hand down the craft. So some of the master makers change their jobs to another. However, few master makers also pass the craft on to next person.

For the sake of handing down the craft, more and more people take the responsibility in Taiwan and China. Some start classed to teach people. Some go to villages to investigate. Some develop this craft as a work of art. They all want to let more people realize the ingenious thought and beauty of the flour miniature.
