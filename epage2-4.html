The master makers are like magicians. They give the dough a new life. Every flour miniature is like a real one. The key factor is the master marker's technique. We can divide into several kinds as below:
Pinch: the thumb and the index finger are as basic point. Other fingers are secondary. (The markers usually make an embryo by pinching.)
Rub: use two hands or put the dough on the table and use one hand to rub the dough into different shapes. "Tang-yuan" (Glutinous Rice Balls) also uses this way to make it.)
Press: use two palms or put the dough on the table and then flatten it. (Using the different strength or method will produce various outcomes. If using different colors, it will be more colorful. We use the way to make the flour miniature's clothes.)
Point: using the fingertips to make small dough and stick to the end items. (Just like eyes. But you should make it carefully. Don't be impatient.)
Stick: to connect small parts to big parts. (Some parts needed to connect are like arms and legs, we stick them to be firm.)
Paste: stick sheet dough to the main body. (Such as clothes and headdress flowers.)
Meteor lines: put small piece of dough on the main body and then use the index finger wipe down the stripe like meteor.
Spiral lines: put the dough of different colors in order. And rub it up and down, right side and left side. Then different colors merge into regular and beautiful lines.
