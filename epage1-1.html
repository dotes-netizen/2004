The oldest art of flour miniature in the world
The source of flour miniature has been not found anymore. But in Xin-jiang in China, people found the “yong of human beings and pigs” which made by the flour in the Tang Dynasty’s tomb. Archeologists speculated that they were made more than 1,340 years ago. This shows that the fountainhead of this folk craft is very early.

The legend in history
People said that in the era of the three kingdoms(A.D.220~260), Zhu Ge-Liang, the army adviser, dispatched the troops to fight with south savages. When they wanted to cross the river, they met a strong wind. The river rose suddenly. They used the miniatures of human beings and domestic animals that made by glutinous rice to worship. Then they can cross the river without a hitch. And this is the source of “man tou” which represents the sound of "savage's head" in Chinese. From now on, people who do this job call Zhu Ge-Liang the founder.
