Basically, we can mix flour, glutinous rice powder, and water and then knead them. It becomes dough. You can follow these tips below, then you can make it easily at home!

The rate of flour and glutinous rice powder is about three to one.
>
Add water slowly. (More in winter, but less in summer.)
>
Knead it until it becomes glutinous dough.
Put slight cloth on the tray of a steamer.
>
Put the dough on the slight cloth.
>
Spread the dough on the slight cloth.
Add alum and then wrap the dough with the slight dough.
>
Cover the lid and add water to boil it. (15~20 minutes)
Take the temperature. Although the dough now is hot, you should rub it because it will become hard while cooling.
>
Rub the cooked dough by hands with eating oil.
>
Press it into the flat dough.
Add pigments.
>
Fold the dough.
>
And push forward by palm.
Repeat the further actions until the dough without white points and the color is uniform.
>
The dough moistens the paper easily. You can use the plastic bag to keep it.
The basic colors are yellow, orange, and blue. You can mix them to become many colors.
The dough on the left side is no pigment and its color is like skin. On the right is the dough with white pigment
.
Before, the pigments are to use plants or minerals. So colors are less. Now the pigments are chemical. So colors are more. Allocating the dough's color is like mixing the watercolors.
If you need the color like skin, you just mix white and pink. Grass green is to mix white, yellow, and green. Purple is to mix blue and pink. Just by this method, we can make many different colors.
But the colors that we seldom use don't make too much. We should seal up the dough we don't use in a plastic bag. That's to keep the dough from hardening. (If the dough hardens, you can put it into a steamer or an electric rice cooker to heat about ten minutes.)
