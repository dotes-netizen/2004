The Background of Mr. Huang
He is an ordinary child just like other children who were born in Hsien-His. His mother has become his father's wife when his mother was a child. He has a talent for studying. He was the leader of class when he studied at the elementary school. His uncle bet a new bike on Mr. Huang's full marks about his exams. He was very proud of himself because he won the bet. (He was also the first child who owned a bike in his village.) He also got the county magistrate's prize when he graduated from the elementary school. He had a chance to enter the private junior high school. But his family was so poor that he should give it up.

Contents of the interview
Caller : Why do you leave your original job?
Mr. Huang : Although the original job is in a big company. But I think human beings should find a job that they are interested in. Then they will work hard for their ideal and interest. I have my own ideal, too. That's why I leave my original job.
Caller : What kind of situation brings you into contact with the flour miniature?
Mr. Huang : In Chinese's cultures, we have to make "Tang-yuan" (Glutinous Rice Balls) in the winter solstice. But we have another custom. We use the flour to make some miniatures of pigs, dogs, and so on. Then we take these miniatures to worship Gods in the stockyard. So since I was a child, I have contacted the flour miniature.
Caller : Who is your irradiative teacher?
Mr. Huang : When I was a child, almost all of my relatives could make the flour miniature. All of them are my irradiative teachers. However, my grandma influences me the most in the traditional craft. And my older brother plays an important role on teaching me the usage of pigment.
Caller : Why do you want to open a class to teach others?
Mr. Huang : Because the flour miniature is a culture of temple fair. In the past, people just bought the flour miniature and then ate it. Now I think it is a kind of art. I hope more and more people can admire it. So I should teach people how to make it. Then people will try to appreciate the art when they see it next time.
Caller : Does any student impress you most in your class?
Mr. Huang : Yes. There is a student who impresses me the most. His name is Lin Yi-Hang, one of your school's students. He is not good-looking and heavy. And he is a grumpy boy. But I find that he can cool himself to concentrate on the flour miniature. Sometimes he does better than I.
Caller : What is your favorite work?
Mr. Huang : In fact, my favorite work is not the thing that I make. It is the children's smiles. Every time when children get the flour miniature from my hand, their happy smiles on their faces are my favorite work. And it can last forever.
Caller : Have you ever gained some rewards?
Mr. Huang : Actually I don't like to join contests. But my students took the first place when they joined contests. As for myself, I don't like to, because...
Caller : You fear for losing!
Mr. Huang : A little bit. But I don't think that people must compete with others. So gaining the rewards or not is no difference for me.
Caller : Is your family against you to do this job?
Mr. Huang : Of course. I gave up the stable job. Many relatives worried how I could support a family. In fact, this is not a problem if we are not greedy and live in a simple life. So I still participate in this job.
Caller : Does the flour miniature have any influence on you?
Mr. Huang : Though the flour miniature, you can tell others what you are thinking and how you feel. When you are excited, your work's countenance will be happy like you. When you are depressed, your work's countenance will be sad like you. That is because your emotion will be shown on the flour miniature. The flour miniature can calm down my anxious and uneasy mood. And the dough is in your hands. It will be what you want it to be.
Caller : Do you have any new breakthrough?
Mr. Huang : In the traditional ways, we just paste, twine, and squash. Now we can use the dough to draw. The dough has high compatibility with paper. And it is opaque for our vision. It is similar to the oil painting. Besides, we generalize several kinds of basic types and let students create by themselves.
Caller : Have you ever undergo some difficulty?
Mr. Huang : In making the flour miniature, we need to face the climate in Taiwan. We must change our material to adapt the climate. In developing this craft, people will commercialize your ideal or criticize you. So no matter how hard it is, the most important is that you must insist on your ideal.
Caller : How do you pass through crisis?
Mr. Huang : I have my own dream and I am consistent in it. My belief helps me pass through crisis.
Caller : What kind of works will you create in the future?
Mr. Huang : Like what I mentioned before, my favorite work is children's smiles. I hope I can see more smiles in the future. Besides, I want to finish a king-size work about ¡§ Eight Gods Ferry the Sea¡¨.
Caller : Do you have any ideals about the flour miniature's future?
Mr. Huang : First, I hope that every Hsien-His villager can make the flour miniature. Maybe some day I will hear that ¡§I can make the flour miniature!¡¨ from Hsien-His villages' mouths. Second, I hope not only Hsien-His villagers but also minded people can learn it.

Postscript
We have interviewed Mr.Huang for several times. Sometimes he showed us how to make the flour miniatures. He always chatted with us about the ancestor's education, history, or evaluation and so on. His studio was not the same as what we thought before. It was just a room in the Chinese traditional rectangular courtyard. All his family from grandparents to children could make the flour miniatures. When we interviewed him, some children would pop their heads in. Let us feel that he was like a ruler of children. Making the flour miniature is like one part of Mr. Huang's life. It is so natural and sincere. We are so lucky to know Mr. Huang of our hometown.
