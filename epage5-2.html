China has over five thousand years' culture. It contains lots of different folk customs and cultures. Many Taiwan's crafts are also passed from China directly or indirectly. To the people foodstuff is all-important. And the flour miniature is a folk art in Chinese agricultural society. So using eatable materials is popular. However, some factors will influence the materials and producing methods. One is the different climates in south China and north China. Then people in north eat wheat and people in south eat rice. The other is the different folk customs.

The difference of making the flour miniature in north and south
The way in making "mian hua"(flowers made of dough)- the flour miniature in north
1. To grind wheat into powder. Then add water, ferment flour and vitriol. Mix them smoothly.
2. Shaping. Basic skills: rub, knead, press, twist, squeeze, imprint and stick. Besides, use the tools like knifes, scissors, combs and so on.
3. Steam in a steamer.
4. Dyeing. One is limpid color that we call "pin se" in Chinese. It includes pink, blue and yellow. The other is opaque color that we call "gao zi se" or "guang gao se" in Chinese. It includes dark blue and white. Add some wine to make the colors well mixed.
5. To separate colors by put some glue (leather glue or bone glue).
6. Then dry in the air.
The way in making "jiang mi ren"(glutinous rice men)- the flour miniature in south
1. Take glutinous rice as the main material. Add little flour and water and then knead to become the dough. The dough's size is like a hand. Put it in the boiling water.
2. After cooking, add salt- to avoid hardening, vitriol- to avoid moisture, antiseptic- avoid mold and mix it well.
3. After cooling, add pigments and divide into several parts.
4. Shaping mostly by fingers and some tools. The skills: rub, imprint, spiral lines, meteor lines, binding and so on. Basic shapes: sphere, water drop appearance, an oblong shape. Then using these basic shapes to vary.

In China, the culture of the flour miniature is popular along the Yellow River. Many areas like Gan-Su, Ning-Xia, Shan-Xi, Inner Mongolia, Ho-Nan, Ho-bei, and Shan-Dong have this custom. Although the names are different in different places, the methods of making the flour miniatures are alike. "man hua"- the scientific name of the flour miniature is called in Shan-Xi. "mian ren ren" is called in north Shan-Xi and Inner Mongolia. "mian yang"is called in Ho-bei. "mian lao hu" is called in Shan-Dong. It is also called "hua gao hun dun" or "guo shi" in other places. Southern people call it "jian mian ren" and "nie mian ren" In Taiwan, it is called "mi guo diao" or "glutinous rice men". We can know the flour miniature is so various and plentiful from the different names.
